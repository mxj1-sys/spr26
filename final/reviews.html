Peterson Field Guide to Birds of Eastern and Central North America

A great beginner’s guide

Overall rating: 4.5/5

This review covers the Peterson Field Guide to Birds of Eastern and Central North America (6th edition), aimed at beginner to intermediate birdwatchers. It is a very beginner friendly text that highlights over 800 bird species native to Eastern and Central North America that can be found for less than $10 on numerous online sites. Given that it is an older edition, it is mostly found on second hand sites.

The book contains numerous features that aid in beginner and/or intermediate bird watcher’s ability to identify different species. The pages are filled with illustrations and detailed drawings depicting both male and female plummages that help the user match birds in the wild to those on the pages. These images also contain arrows that highlight field marks for easier identification.

In the back of the book, you can find silhouettes of different birds as well as all of the birds listed in the guide that you can check off on your life list. The text is concise enough that one can easily read it on the trail. The book itself is quite durable and can survive being thrown in a backpack quickly. On several of my bird watching excursions, I had to throw the guide in my backpack in a rush to get a good look at a bird. The book didn't crease. The cover is quite sturdy. In addition, the book contains range maps that can help identify locations where specific birds are normally found.

On my latest bird watching adventure, I was able to identify five new bird species using the guide. The illustrations really stand out since they also compare the bird sizes to recognizable birds like robin size or hawk size.

However, the guide is slightly bulkier than other guides such as the Sibley Guide or even other guides by Peterson. In addition, some of the birds such as the gull depictions do not illustrate all of the plumage variations which can make it harder to identify different bird variations. In a similar aspect, the book can be considered incomplete as the always changing weather and climate patterns cause changes in the bird migration patterns. In my bird watching class, my professor did criticize the book for this issue. However, this is an issue across all guides as they are constantly becoming outdated as migration patterns change.

Being a book that was published in 2010, some of the data can be outdated. I found that the book is missing some birds that are now native to the region and feature birds that are more rare or even unlikely to be found in the region anymore. The book is definitely more geared towards the beginner level bird watchers. Those that are more familiar with the birds in the area may find the illustrations too basic for more trickier species. Similarly, it lacks detailed behavioral notes that advanced bird watchers may want.

Overall,this guide is recommended for beginner and intermediate birders. Although advanced bird watchers may prefer a more detailed guide, the fairly cheap price and easy identification make this a solid choice for casual birdwatchers.

Osprey Farpoint Fairview Travel 15L Daypack

The perfect travel companion

Overall rating: 4/5

This review covers the Osprey Farpoint Fairview Travel 15L Daypack and its use as a lightweight pack for birdwatching. The backpack itself is 7.87H X 11.02W X 18.5D in and stores up to 15L . It is a very lightweight option for a small trip birdwatching as it only weighs 1.2lbs. The backpack can be found in numerous colors on many outdoor gear sites such as Osprey itself or REI.

What sets this backpack apart from other similar ones is that it is so lightweight and compact . When you are out birdwatching, you want to have minimal gear to move quickly and quietly through different terrains so as to not disturb the birds. This backpack is perfect for this. I have brought this backpack on all of my bird watching missions.

In addition, the Osprey brand is known for making very durable and long lasting backpacks that withstand a lot of travel. This backpack surely does not disappoint in that area. I even brought this backpack all across international travel into China and you couldn’t even tell. There are numerous pouches that can store any bird watching guides you need, binoculars, or small snacks. On my last bird watching trip, I was able to bring two water bottles, my Peterson field guide, numerous small snacks, a pair of binoculars, sunglasses, and sunscreen and I didn't feel like any of the weight or bulkiness that I do with other backpacks. This is further reinforced by a strap that comes across the chest area of the user that helps to distribute the weight of the bag and prevent it from slipping too low.

The bag itself is actually part of a set that attaches to a larger travel bag. As such, there are some unnecessary zippers that would be used to attach the bag. In addition, the side water bottle pockets fit well for small bottled waters, but for larger metal water bottles, you may have difficulty fitting them.

More advanced bird watchers who may have numerous guides or other birdwatching gear may find that the area inside doesn’t provide enough compartments for separation. There is one main divider inside that can be used to store a laptop or books and then the main compartment that holds everything else. Given that the backpack is meant to be a lightweight small option, some users may find that it is too small and doesn’t carry larger bulkier items as well.

Overall, the Osprey Farpoint Fairview Travel 15L Daypack is a perfect addition for beginner and intermediate bird watchers who need a small pack. It has the perfect mixture of being lightweight yet sturdy. It has enough storage space to hold the few small items that one would need to bring on a small birdwatching excursion and at the price of $39, it is cheaper than competitive backpacks while not compromising quality.
