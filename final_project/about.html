About the Authors

Rose

Rose is interested in bird behavior, local ecosystems, and how everyday places can become meaningful birdwatching sites. She was the primary writer of the site. She also scouted out perfect bird watching spots at Rutgers. She is excited to continue writing more features on birding expectation vs reality, as well as adding to the bird of the month page.

Amethyst

Amethyst focuses on visual storytelling, perception, and how people assign meaning to birds in urban environments. She was in charge of all of the visual aspects of the website. As such, she was took all of the amazing pictures you see on the site! She looks foward to continuing to add additional images to the site. Keep an eye out for more to come!

Jade

Jade is interested in conservation, endangered bird species, and the relationship between science and public awareness. She verified all of the facts about the different birds and did most of the research on the reviewed products. She is very passionate about making sure the site has all the up to date information on each bird. Keep checking back to learn more!

Project process

Website Timeline

1. Choosing the Topic - April 2025

We all met at a bird watching festival. We began talking about creating a website to share our passion for birding. We began thinking about how we could create a unique space with a different perspective than other birding sites.

2. Collecting Images and Observations - May 2025

We photographed birds and birding locations around Rutgers to build the visual foundation of the website. It was here that we started encapsulating the main idea of the site...changing human perception of birds as a whole.

3. Mapping Birding Locations - December 2025

Amethyst created an interactive map to connect Rutgers campuses with specific birdwatching spots that Rose had picked out. Jade made sure to verify that the birds listed in this section were birds that could be seen in these spots.

4. Building the Final Website - April 2026

We organized the different pages into one complete website called In Flight. The site launched April 2026. We continue to add more pages and perfect the website. We want to create a safe space for people to re-evaluate their biases against and for certain birds.
